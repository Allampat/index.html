A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Great Indian scientist and politician who played a leading role in the development of india's missile and nuclear weapons and programs.

About the Legend

  • Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India, was a renowned scientist and the 11th President of India.
  • He graduated in Physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, and studied aerospace engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology.
  • Kalam worked at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), where he was instrumental in developing India's ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology, earning him the nickname "Missile Man of India."
  • He played a key role in India's 1998 nuclear tests, which established the country as a nuclear power.
  • As President from 2002 to 2007, he was affectionately known as the "People's President." Kalam was a strong advocate for transforming India into a knowledge superpower and developed the "India Vision 2020" plan.
  • He received numerous accolades, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award. An inspirational figure, he authored several books, such as "Wings of Fire."
  • Abdul Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong, leaving behind a legacy that continues to inspire.
